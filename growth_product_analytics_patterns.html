Business Scenario

A subscription ecommerce app wants to understand whether growth is healthy. The team tracks users, product events, orders, and marketing spend, then turns those raw tables into metrics for product, lifecycle, and growth teams.

Raw Tablesusers, events, orders, marketing_spend.
Metric TablesDaily active users, cohort retention, channel LTV/CAC, payback.
End UsersProduct managers, growth marketers, analysts, founders, finance.

End Goal

Answer the real operating questions: are users coming back, are they converting, are channels profitable, and where should the team invest next?

EngagementDAU, MAU, DAU/MAU, power users.
RevenueLTV, CTV, ARPU, ARPPU, payback.
RetentionDay-N retention, churn, resurrection.

Use Cases

  • Product health: DAU, MAU, stickiness.
  • Funnels: signup to purchase or activation.
  • Retention: cohort matrices and churn.
  • Revenue: LTV, CTV, CAC, payback.

How It Saves Money

  • Find weak channels: stop buying low-LTV users.
  • Fix leaks: identify funnel drop-offs early.
  • Improve payback: know when spend returns.
  • Reduce ad waste: compare CAC to retained value.

When To Use It

  • You have user signups and recurring behavior.
  • Marketing channels need ROI measurement.
  • Retention matters more than one-time traffic.
  • Revenue can be tied to users or accounts.

When Not To Use It

  • Your tracking events are unreliable.
  • You cannot join users to orders or spend.
  • The product has no repeat usage motion.
  • You need causal answers but only have descriptive data.

Metric Glossary: Full Form, Definition, Why It Matters

Use this as the quick translation layer from business question to SQL metric. The formulas are intentionally simple; real companies usually add filters for bots, refunds, internal users, test accounts, and data quality rules.

Metric Full Form Plain-English Definition Why It Matters Common Trap
DAU Daily Active Users Unique users who performed a meaningful active event on a day. Shows daily product usage and short-term health. Counting weak events like page load can inflate activity.
WAU Weekly Active Users Unique users active during a calendar week or rolling 7-day window. Smooths noisy daily usage and shows weekly habit strength. Calendar week and rolling 7-day WAU are different.
MAU Monthly Active Users Unique users active during a month or rolling 30-day window. Shows reach and top-level audience size. MAU can grow while engagement quality gets worse.
DAU/MAU Daily Active Users divided by Monthly Active Users The share of monthly users who are active on an average day. Measures stickiness and habit frequency. High stickiness is only good if the active event reflects real value.
Activation Rate Activated Users divided by Signups Percent of new users who reach a key value moment, such as first purchase or first project created. Shows whether new users understand and experience the product's value. Choosing the wrong activation event makes the metric useless.
Retention Day-N or Cohort Retention Percent of users from a signup cohort who come back after N days. Shows whether growth is durable or just leaky acquisition. Exact-day retention and rolling retention answer different questions.
Churn Churned Users / Churn Rate Users who stop being active or stop paying during a period. Shows product leakage and revenue risk. Free-user churn and paid-user churn should not be mixed casually.
Resurrection Resurrected Users Users who were inactive for a long gap and then returned. Shows win-back, lifecycle, or seasonality effects. Short inactivity windows can mistake normal usage gaps for resurrection.
ARPU Average Revenue Per User Total revenue divided by total users or active users. Shows monetization efficiency across the user base. Be explicit whether denominator is all users, active users, or signups.
ARPPU Average Revenue Per Paying User Total revenue divided by users who paid. Shows monetization depth among buyers. Can rise while total revenue falls if only a few whales remain.
LTV Lifetime Value Total or predicted revenue/profit a user creates over their lifetime. Used to decide how much you can afford to spend acquiring users. Revenue LTV and profit LTV are not the same.
CTV Cumulative Transaction Value Running total of a user's transaction revenue over time. Shows how user value builds purchase by purchase. Do not confuse it with predicted LTV; CTV is historical so far.
CAC Customer Acquisition Cost Marketing spend divided by acquired customers or users. Shows how expensive growth is by channel. Blended CAC can hide one very bad channel.
LTV/CAC Lifetime Value divided by Customer Acquisition Cost How much value you get back for each acquisition dollar. Helps decide whether a channel is profitable enough to scale. A ratio can look good while payback is too slow for cash flow.
Payback Period CAC Payback Period Number of days or months until cumulative revenue covers acquisition cost. Shows how fast the business recovers marketing spend. Gross revenue payback is optimistic if margins are low.
Funnel Conversion Step-to-step Conversion Rate Percent of users who move from one product step to the next. Shows where users drop off before reaching value or purchase. Steps must be ordered by timestamp, not just counted independently.

-- TABLE 1: users.
-- One row per person/account. This tells us where the user came from.
create table users (
    user_id bigint primary key,
    signup_date date,
    acquisition_channel text,
    country text,
    plan text
);

-- TABLE 2: events.
-- One row per product action. This tells us what the user did.
create table events (
    event_id text primary key,
    user_id bigint,
    event_name text,
    event_timestamp timestamp,
    event_date date
);

-- TABLE 3: orders.
-- One row per purchase. This tells us how much money the user created.
create table orders (
    order_id text primary key,
    user_id bigint,
    order_date date,
    revenue numeric
);

-- TABLE 4: marketing_spend.
-- One row per channel/day. This tells us how much we paid to acquire users.
create table marketing_spend (
    spend_date date,
    acquisition_channel text,
    spend numeric
);

-- TABLE 5: dashboard output.
-- Like a 10-year-old version:
-- "One row per day with the important product scoreboard numbers."
create table daily_growth_metrics (
    metric_date date primary key,
    dau integer,
    new_users integer,
    paying_users integer,
    revenue numeric,
    arpu numeric
);

-- Query 1: build the daily product scoreboard.
insert into daily_growth_metrics (
    metric_date,
    dau,
    new_users,
    paying_users,
    revenue,
    arpu
)
with dau as (
    -- Count unique active users per day.
    select
        event_date,
        count(distinct user_id) as dau
    from events
    where event_name in ('app_open', 'view_product', 'purchase')
    group by event_date
),
new_users as (
    -- Count how many users signed up each day.
    select
        signup_date,
        count(*) as new_users
    from users
    group by signup_date
),
revenue as (
    -- Count who paid and how much money came in each day.
    select
        order_date,
        count(distinct user_id) as paying_users,
        sum(revenue) as revenue
    from orders
    group by order_date
)
select
    d.event_date as metric_date,
    d.dau,
    coalesce(n.new_users, 0) as new_users, -- If nobody signed up, show zero.
    coalesce(r.paying_users, 0) as paying_users,
    coalesce(r.revenue, 0) as revenue,
    round(coalesce(r.revenue, 0) / nullif(d.dau, 0), 2) as arpu -- revenue per active user
from dau d
left join new_users n
    on d.event_date = n.signup_date
left join revenue r
    on d.event_date = r.order_date;

-- Query 2: LTV, CAC, and LTV/CAC by channel.
-- Like a 10-year-old version:
-- "How much are users worth, and how much did it cost to get them?"
with user_ltv as (
    select
        u.user_id,
        u.acquisition_channel,
        coalesce(sum(o.revenue), 0) as lifetime_revenue
    from users u
    left join orders o
        on u.user_id = o.user_id
    group by u.user_id, u.acquisition_channel
),
channel_ltv as (
    select
        acquisition_channel,
        avg(lifetime_revenue) as avg_ltv
    from user_ltv
    group by acquisition_channel
),
channel_cac as (
    select
        u.acquisition_channel,
        sum(m.spend) / nullif(count(distinct u.user_id), 0) as cac
    from users u
    left join marketing_spend m
        on u.signup_date = m.spend_date
       and u.acquisition_channel = m.acquisition_channel
    group by u.acquisition_channel
)
select
    l.acquisition_channel,
    round(l.avg_ltv, 2) as avg_ltv,
    round(c.cac, 2) as cac,
    round(l.avg_ltv / nullif(c.cac, 0), 2) as ltv_cac_ratio
from channel_ltv l
join channel_cac c
    on l.acquisition_channel = c.acquisition_channel
order by ltv_cac_ratio desc;

More SQL Patterns: Retention, CTV, Funnel

These are the next patterns product teams usually ask for once the daily scoreboard exists.

-- Retention matrix.
-- Like a 10-year-old version:
-- "For each signup day, check who came back after 1 day, 7 days, 30 days."
with cohorts as (
    select
        user_id,
        signup_date as cohort_date
    from users
),
activity as (
    select distinct
        user_id,
        event_date
    from events
    where event_name in ('app_open', 'view_product', 'purchase')
),
cohort_activity as (
    select
        c.cohort_date,
        a.event_date - c.cohort_date as days_since_signup,
        c.user_id
    from cohorts c
    join activity a
        on c.user_id = a.user_id
    where a.event_date >= c.cohort_date
)
select
    cohort_date,
    days_since_signup,
    count(distinct user_id) as retained_users
from cohort_activity
where days_since_signup between 0 and 30
group by cohort_date, days_since_signup
order by cohort_date, days_since_signup;

-- CTV: cumulative transaction value.
-- Like a 10-year-old version:
-- "Keep adding each user's orders as time passes."
select
    user_id,
    order_id,
    order_date,
    revenue,
    sum(revenue) over (
        partition by user_id
        order by order_date, order_id
        rows between unbounded preceding and current row
    ) as cumulative_transaction_value
from orders
order by user_id, order_date, order_id;

-- Funnel conversion.
-- Like a 10-year-old version:
-- "How many people made it from step 1 to step 2 to step 3?"
with user_funnel as (
    select
        user_id,
        min(event_timestamp) filter (where event_name = 'app_open') as app_open_at,
        min(event_timestamp) filter (where event_name = 'view_product') as view_product_at,
        min(event_timestamp) filter (where event_name = 'add_to_cart') as add_to_cart_at,
        min(event_timestamp) filter (where event_name = 'purchase') as purchase_at
    from events
    group by user_id
)
select
    count(*) filter (where app_open_at is not null) as app_open_users,
    count(*) filter (where view_product_at >= app_open_at) as view_product_users,
    count(*) filter (where add_to_cart_at >= view_product_at) as add_to_cart_users,
    count(*) filter (where purchase_at >= add_to_cart_at) as purchase_users
from user_funnel;
